QUARTERLY ENGINEERING UPDATE — Q2
Platform Reliability Team

SUMMARY

This quarter the Platform Reliability team focused on three things: cutting the
median API latency, reducing on-call burden, and finishing the migration off the
legacy job scheduler. Two of the three goals were met in full; the scheduler
migration is at 80 percent and will carry into next quarter.

LATENCY

Median API response time dropped from 480 ms to 210 ms after we introduced a
read-through cache in front of the primary database and moved image thumbnailing
to a background worker. The 95th-percentile latency, which is the number most
visible to customers, fell from 1.9 s to 720 ms. We consider the latency goal
fully met. The one regression we are watching is cold-start latency on the
thumbnailing workers, which can spike to 4 s after a deploy; a warm-pool fix is
scheduled for early next quarter.

ON-CALL

Paging volume fell by 41 percent quarter over quarter, from an average of 22
pages per week to 13. Most of that reduction came from two changes: we raised the
alert threshold on a noisy disk-usage check that was firing on transient spikes,
and we automated the most common remediation — restarting a stuck consumer —
which previously required a human at 3 a.m. The team agreed that anything below
10 pages per week is the target for next quarter.

SCHEDULER MIGRATION

The migration from the legacy cron-based scheduler to the new queue-driven system
is 80 percent complete. All recurring jobs have been moved; what remains are
roughly 30 ad-hoc jobs owned by other teams, which require coordination before
they can be cut over. The hard deadline for full migration is the end of next
quarter, because the legacy scheduler's host is being decommissioned at that
time. The main risk is that the dependent teams deprioritize the work, so we are
escalating to engineering leadership to get the remaining jobs scheduled.

NEXT QUARTER

Priorities for next quarter are: finish the scheduler migration, ship the
worker warm-pool fix, and bring weekly paging volume under 10. We will also begin
a review of our backup-restore procedure, which has not been exercised in over a
year.
